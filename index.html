Existential nihilism can be defined as the philosophical position that there is no intrinsic value or meaning to life.8 Regarding life within the universe, existential nihilists believe that human life is insignificant and without purpose. Therefore, it is up to individuals themselves to create meaning in their lives through their own freedom of choice. Existential nihilism is commonly associated with destructive, impulsive behavior and suicide.7 However, more recently, it has seen a resurgence in mainstream culture and contemporary work. It is important to note that while existential nihilism does overlap with existentialism, it is not the same thing but a different (yet similar) branch of philosophical thought. For instance, existentialism deals with the ways to address the lack of intrinsic meaning in the world.
